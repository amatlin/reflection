this site analyzes itself.

Every click, every page view, every second you spend here is captured, stored, transformed, and displayed back to you in real time.

There is no product. There is no content. There is only the infrastructure — pointed at itself.

This exhibit walks through how the data pipeline works. You'll fire a real event and watch it travel through the system.

the loop

When you click the button below, a fire_event is captured by the analytics tracker, sent to the backend, stored in the database, and broadcast to every connected visitor.

Watch the stream on the right.

the warehouse

Every hour, events are exported from the live database to BigQuery. The warehouse on the right lets you run real SQL against the public dataset.

Try a query — click one of the chips in the warehouse panel on the right.

the pipeline

A dbt pipeline transforms the raw warehouse data into staging tables and daily metric aggregations — pageviews, unique visitors, events per session.

The analytics panel on the right shows today's metrics. Click one of the question chips to ask a question — it gets translated to SQL and run against the warehouse.

the model

A real data stack doesn't stop at metrics. It builds models — patterns extracted from the data and reflected back as insight.

This step will analyze what visitors have written in the questionnaire: topic clusters, sentiment, and themes that emerge from the collected responses. The modeling panel on the right is where those results will appear.

Content coming soon.

the apparatus

Every tech company builds this machinery to track, measure, and optimize human behavior. This site strips it down to the bare core: the apparatus, pointed at nothing but itself.
